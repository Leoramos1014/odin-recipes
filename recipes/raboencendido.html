Rabo Encendido

A delicious and flavorful meal

A traditional Cuban ox tail stew that's slow cooked in wine and beer until the meat is fall off the bone tender. Traditionally served with white rice, plantains and a watercress salad.

Ingredients

  • 2 kg Oxtail
  • 2 Bay leaves
  • 2 Carrots
  • 2 Celery sticks
  • 4 Garlic cloves
  • 2 Onions
  • 60 g Parsley
  • 1 kg Tomatoes
  • 30 ml Vegetable oil
  • 750 ml Red wine
  • 250 ml Sherry, dry

Step By Step Instructions

    Don’t let the long cooking time scare you! Do you have to carve out a few hours in your day to make this oxtail soup recipe? Yes. Is a lot of it hands-off? Also, yes. Is it worth it? Absolutely!

  1. Prep the oxtail & sofrito
  2. Brown the oxtail. Generously season all sides of the oxtail with salt and pepper.
  3. In a Dutch oven, heat oil at medium-high heat. Brown the oxtail on all sides. You may have to do this in batches.
  4. Remove from pot and set aside.
  5. Sauté the green peppers and onions for 1-2 minutes or until tender.
  6. Add the garlic and sauté for 30 seconds. Stir in the beef broth, cooking wine, tomato sauce, complete seasoning, cumin, bay leaves, salt + pepper to taste.
  7. After an hour, gently stir the meat. If you notice the pot needs liquid (or drying out), add 1 cup of water.
  8. Continue to cook for 1 more hour at low heat until the meat is soft and fork-tender.
  9. After 2 hours, add in the carrots and potatoes.
  10. Cook for another 30 minutes until tender.

    11. If you pierce the meat with a knife and it’s tender, it’s done and ready. Just make sure the carrots and potatoes are tender too.Taste and adjust seasonings.
